01 — Vision

Queens of the Past: A Visual Archaeology

This project stems from a personal obsession: a fascination with archaeology and the Orientalist painting of masters such as Jean-Léon Gérôme. This time, the focus lies on the great queens of the past — women of power whose legacies I have sought to reimagine through my own creative lens.

My process is a ritual of stratification. I blend the ancient allure of Arabic and Hebrew calligraphy and Egyptian hieroglyphs with the tactile textures of damasks and natural elements. I employ collage as my primary medium, a space where the analogue meets the digital, incorporating bespoke elements crafted through Artificial Intelligence.

It is my way of navigating uncharted visual territories, where millennial history engages in a dialogue with the technologies of the future.

02 — The Works

Four Sovereigns

03 — The Queens

Dihya "Khaina"
The Oracle of the Aurès

A study on North African sovereignty and materiality. The earth-cracked textures reflect the resilience of the Zenata tribes, while the crimson mantle serves as a chromatic focal point against a desaturated, timeless void. The suspension of geometric spheres acts as a visual punctuation, breaking the historical narrative to remind the viewer of the artifice.

Nefertiti
The Sun of Amarna

Immortal elegance through a modernist lens. This collage fuses the perfection of the Egyptian canon with geometric abstractions. The work focuses on the profile as a monumental silhouette, where the textured crown becomes a vessel for ancient pigments and digital noise.

Zenobia
The Ambition of Palmyra

A reconstruction of power at the edge of the Roman Empire. This piece explores the balance between the predator's gaze and diplomatic grace. The integration of traditional calligraphy and floral patterns symbolizes the cultural syncretism of a queen who turned the desert into a golden crossroads of civilization.

Tamara
The King of Georgia

Warrior and Saint. The composition focuses on the martial sacrality of the Georgian Queen. The golden halo acts as a structural anchor, contrasting with the metallic textures of the armor and the deep reds of medieval iconography, evoking the rugged beauty of the Caucasus.

04 — Method & Process

The Digital Fresco Technique

This project began as a personal investigation into the reconstruction of historical female archetypes through a technique I call digital-fresco. It is not simply digital art — it is a layered and complex working method that builds a bridge between past and future.

The process moves through three distinct registers: analogue research into historical textures and symbols drawn from primary sources; manual work through digital painting and meticulous composition; and emerging technology, integrating AI-generated elements to add new layers of meaning to the image.

In the age of algorithms, the role of the artist remains to orchestrate different tools in order to construct visual narratives that are both strategically grounded and aesthetically resonant.

"Four women who shaped history — reconstructed in the language of the present."
